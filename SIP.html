Compost is an excellent organic material that can be used as a fertilizer or to amend the soil. Compost helps balance soil density, aids in the plants' ability to absorb water, prevents plants from drying out as quickly, and gives them resistance to disease and pests.

Malabar Spinach, also known locally as Alugbati or Basella alba L. is the scientific name for this herbaceous vine. For optimal growth, it requires sunlight, soil, and plenty of water. Phosphorus, protein, vitamin A, and iron are also required.

Homemade compost is created using a wide range of materials under aerobic compost. Fruit and vegetable scraps, coffee grounds, eggshells, grass and plant clippings are all required. Phosphorus also helps condition and neutralize your soil, which increases phosphorus availability.

Benefits of aerobic compost include a significant reduction in valuable waste stream going off island, organics, soil, and water remaining on island. It also improves soil structure and provides much-needed nutrients for plants.

The effective way to apply and use the compost is to spread the compost in a thick layer on top of exposed soil. This would help the compost to meld with the soil. It could help the plant to be healthy because it would contain the plant's natural nutrients.


SIP Project Title - The Value Of Composting

Problem - Will our Alugbati plant grow an abundance in the number of leaves and the diameter of the base leaves if we use a large amount of compost?

Hypothesis - If we use compost that contains or is high on phosphorus, then our Alugbati plant will grow in the number of leaves and the diameter of the base leaves.


Independent Variable:


- The compost and compost ingredients.


Dependent Variable:


- Number of leaves on our plant.

- Size and or diameter of the base leaves of our plant .


Controlled Variable:


- Amount of compost added to the fertilizer.

- Amount of water.

- The soil used for the Malabar spinach plant.
